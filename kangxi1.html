Ancient Chinese coins: A brief history of Kangxi coins

The KangXi Tong Bao was the official cash coin issued durimg the reign of Emperor KangXi. Like its predecessor only 1 cash coins were issued during his entire 60 years reign. KanXi coins were generally well made and of good quality reflecting the strength of the Chinese economy prvailing dueing the period. Emperor KangXi is one of of China's most famous rulers, known for his long reign, territorial expansion and stable governance. The reverse of the coins indicates the name or location of the mint. Coins minted in Beijing by the Board of Revenue (BoR) and the Board of Works (BoW) bears only manchu scripts while those minted by the other mints feature both a Manchu character on the left and a Chinese character on the right. The KangXi Tong Bao is very popular among collectors because the Chinese characters; "Kang" "Xi" are considered as auspicious as it literally means " Peaceful Prosperity" or "Transquil Harmony".
