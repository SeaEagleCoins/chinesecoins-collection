Ancient Chinese coins: A brief history of Qian Long coins

The QianLong Tong Bao was the official cash coin issued during the reign of Emperor QianLong. Like its predecessors, only 1 cash coins were issued during his entire reign except those minted in Xinjiang. 5 and 10 cash cash coins were issued and used mainly to finance the military campaigns in Xinjiang. QianLong coins were generally well made and of good quality reflecting the strength of the Chinese economy prevailing during the period. Emperor QianLong was a capable ruler. The empire had the largest population and economy in the world and reached its greatest territorial extent during the entire Qing Dynasty. To support the demand of its population and huge economy, millions of cash coins were issued by mints all over its empire, making the Qian Long casg coins the most common among all Qing Dynasty coins. The reverse of the coin indicates the name or location of the mint in Manchu scripts. Easy accessibilty and affordability together with a wide range of different mint marks makes QianLong coins very popular among collectors.
