Ancient Chinese coins: A brief history of Qin coins

The 12 zhu Ban Liang coins were considered by many historians as the 1st unified currency in the imperial dynasty of China. It was mandated by the famous Emperor Qin Shi Huang who was known as the 1st imperial emperor to unify China. The Qin Ban Liang is equivalent to half a teal (also known as a liang) or the weight of 12 zhu (the zhu, like liang, is a unit of weight used in China). The weight of 1 zhu is about 0.6gm. The weight and dimension of Qin BanLiang varies quite a fair bit. Typically it is at least about 7gm in weight with a diameter of 30mm or more. The Qin BanLiang coins are much more scarcer than the 4 zhu and 8 zhu Ban Liang coins issued during the subsequent Han Dynasty.
