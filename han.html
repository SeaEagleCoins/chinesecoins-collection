Ancient Chinese coins: A brief history of Han coins

The Han Dynasty elm leaf Ban Liang was minted as a replacement of the Qin Ban Liang. The Qin coin was considered too heavy by the Han Emperor Gao Zhu. The Han coin, elm leaf Ban Liang is distinct for its small size and light weight. The Han Wu Zhu coin, commissioned during the reign of Han Wu Ti in 119BC became the longest circulated coin in Chinese numismatic history, lasting about 739 years till its withdrawal during the Sui and Tang Dynasty.
